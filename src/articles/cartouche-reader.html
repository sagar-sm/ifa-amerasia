To my benevolent reader

In earlier years, benevolent reader, when I had illustrated the Poeticon Astronomicon of Gaius Julius Hyginus with images of the constellations brought into shape according to my calculation, at the same time geographical delineations had been outlined and marked by me. Claudius Ptolemy had supplied some of these, and others were supplied by his commentator Johannes Werner. Indeed, I took note that Claudius Ptolemy had taught that the ratio of the whole latitude to the whole longitude must be maintained not only in the parallel which should be drawn through Rhodes or Thule, but clearly in all of them. Knowing this ratio, a great deal of related things that had previously been obscure became clear to me, and this specification was among them. Once I had produced copious examples for friends, they urged me, almost daily, to publish them and to promise that I would not hesitate to gratify their wishes, should more certain information about the westerly lands reach me. As the unconquerable Charles the Fifth, Emperor of Rome, forever Augustus, was returning to Cologne, I was wisely striking up a friendship with the Castilians well-known for their substantial expertise, who among others were reporting that Baccalaria, Florida, New Spain, and America have boundaries joined with the eastern edges in this hemisphere. Moreover, none of these [Castilians] were able to speak soundly about the matter except for the Emperor, to whom day in and day out they sent back stories from New Spain, speaking in this way about newly discovered scattered islands.

But then, since it came into consideration (for I had also decided to discuss this matter with his Imperial Majesty ), as I was convening with his Imperial Majesty when investigating these facts, mention was made about these lands recently discussed, and in the midst of this his Imperial Majesty responded that those aforementioned countries were in no way separated by the interval of a sea, but rather connected to the eastern countries. He continued, saying that earlier the Spaniards had again and again gone west exploring from Tenochtitlan, the famous city of the Great Khan, also called Montezuma, and had not been able to find for him an end to the huge land. This would be sufficient proof that the western country reaches to the Chinese region and to the people of China, and make it certain that they belong in the sphere of the Castilians. This information he had received from Indians who had fought in wars with the unconquerable Ferdinand Cortes in New Spain.

I would have quoted more of this statement here, but not for the fact that the book on Cosmography will have a passage about such things when it is eventually brought out, everything, therefore dear reader, that was communicated to me by his Imperial Majesty, and I wish for it to be communicated to you as it was spoken thus.  I offer this upon my arms, and in the meantime it will be of use to you, as you receive it from the mind that was my own source. Furthermore, if you do not delight in these first iterations, then I will publish another drawing depicting a cordiform map. [1]

[1] Cordis humani efigiem: a likeness of a human heart.
